What is Big Data?

Big Data is a scary term that gets thrown around a lot, but what does it really mean?

big data n. Computing data of a very large size, typically to the extent that
its manipulation and management present significant logistical challenges

© Oxford English Dictionary

We're here to show you that as scary as that sounds, it can be used for good.

Recalls

Companies and advertisers seem to already keep track of our every purchase. But this information could also be applied to recalls. Be informed immediately if something you bought has been recalled and instantly get in contact with the manufacturer.

Medical Products

How do you know if a medical product is a good one? The Food and Drug Administration has recently begun working on the Sentinel, a database tracking positive and adverse effects of different medical products, such as vaccines and oral hypoglycemic agents.

Healthcare Providers

Wouldn’t you want to know all you could about a doctor before seeing them? A linked system of healthcare providers could give more information is regards to malpractice and treatment quality. In the American system of health insurance, this would make it even more crucial for providers to be efficient and effective.

Weather

Predicting the weather with accuracy is difficult, but big data could help. One app, WeatherSignal, “crowdsources” weather information and cellphone sensor readings (such as temperature, pressure, and humidity) to create better weather predictions.
